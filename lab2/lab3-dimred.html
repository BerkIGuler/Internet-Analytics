In [64]:
def construct_movie_tag_M():
    """
        params:
            None
        return:
            colum_index2movie_ID: list object, contains the movieID for each column in movie_tag_M
            movie_tag_M: np.array, MxN matrix
    """
    column_index2movie_ID = []
    
    movie_tag_M = np.zeros((num_tags, num_tagged_movies))
    
    for i in range(num_tagged_movies):
        column_index2movie_ID.append(int(relevance_arr[i*1128, 2]))
        movie_tag_M[:, i] = relevance_arr[i*1128 : (i + 1)*1128, 0]
        
    return movie_tag_M, column_index2movie_ID

def find_pcs_var(eigenvalues):
    i = 0
    while (np.sum(eigenvalues[:i]) < sum_eigen * 2/3):
        i += 1
        
    return i + 1

Discussion¶

  • We discovered previously that first PC (x-axis in plot) embeds Quality/Oscar worthiness as its concept.
  • We also discovered that second PC (y-axis in plot) embeds action blockbuster as its concept.

  • For example the movie "Star Wars" and "Edge of Tomorrow" are action movies and they are located at the bottom, which means they have the lowest values in second PC while the movies "Take Care of My Cat" and "Wild Strawberries are more emotional and so have higher values in second PC.

  • As for the first PC, movies like City of God, Fight Club, American Beauty which are sort of cult/classic movies have great quality so higher values in first PC while "Miss Congenaility" and "Just My Luck" have opposite properties and lower values in first PC.

  • We can observe that rotten tomatos score is correlated with both of the PCs.
  • Along the first PC (x-axis), we see a increasing rotten tomato score as we get closer to right part of the plot.
  • Along the second PC (y-axis), we see a slightly increasing rotten tomato score as we get closer to bottom part of the plot.

  • However, the correlation between the second PC and rotten tomato score is weaker.

  • This is quite intuitive because as the quality/oscar worthiness of a movie increases its rotten tomato score is expected to increase. On the other hand, a movie's consisting more action vs. being emotional does not neccessarily correlate positively/negatively with its quality/oscar worthiness

Discussion¶

We observe that the t-SNE plot is much more centered and uniformly distributed than the PCA plot. Furthemore the tags and their corresponding concepts are much better grouped together.
If we hover over the part of our PCA plot which is the most dense (around coordinate (2,-2)) we see that the concepts/genres are much more mixed. For instance we find horror right next to romantic comedy, while for the t-SNE plot the tags of the neighbours of a point are always much more coherent with those of the point - for instance around a coordinate with horror tag we will find more horror movies rather than a romantic comedy.
Thus the clusters of the t-SNE plot are much more interpretable.

This happens because t-SNE is able to preserve the structure of the high dimensional data better than PCA - points which are close to each other in the high-dimensional data will tend to be close to each other in the reduced dimension. This is because, as seen in the course, the t-SNE algorithm will try to preserve nearest-neighbor relationships as much as possible.
